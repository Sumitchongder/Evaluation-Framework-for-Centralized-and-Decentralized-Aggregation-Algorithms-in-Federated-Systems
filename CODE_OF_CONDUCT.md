# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to providing a professional, respectful, and inclusive environment for researchers, practitioners, and contributors participating in this project.

This repository supports **academic research in Federated Learning**, and all interactions should reflect scholarly integrity, technical rigor, and mutual respect.

---

## Expected Behavior

Contributors are expected to:

- Engage respectfully in discussions and reviews
- Provide constructive, evidence-based feedback
- Acknowledge differing viewpoints and methodologies
- Follow ethical research and citation practices
- Maintain professionalism in all communications

---

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are not tolerated:

- Harassment, discrimination, or personal attacks
- Plagiarism or misrepresentation of research
- Deliberate falsification of experimental results
- Publishing private data or violating dataset licenses
- Disruptive, misleading, or abusive conduct

---

## Research Integrity

Given the academic nature of this repository, contributors must:

- Ensure experimental claims are reproducible
- Clearly distinguish empirical results from hypotheses
- Avoid cherry-picking results without justification
- Respect dataset licenses and usage constraints

---

## Enforcement

Maintainers may remove content or restrict participation if this Code of Conduct is violated. Repeated or severe violations may result in permanent exclusion.

---

## Reporting Concerns

Concerns regarding behavior or integrity can be reported via:
- A private GitHub issue, or
- Direct contact with the repository maintainer

All reports will be handled confidentially and fairly.

---

## Attribution

This Code of Conduct is informed by the Contributor Covenant and adapted for academic and research-driven open-source projects.
